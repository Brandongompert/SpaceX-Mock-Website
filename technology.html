Technology

Falcon 9

Falcon 9 is our 2-stage partially reusable rocket, powered by Merlin engines with cryogenic liquid oxygen and rocket-grade kerosene as fuel. Falcon 9 is unique because each rocket has a reusable first stage, and we have rockets that have flown over 10 missions. It has delivered hundreds of payloads into orbit, recovering over 100 first stage boosters, and that number is rapidly increasing every year.

Falcon Heavy

Falcon Heavy is currently the most powerful rocket commercially available in the world. Falcon Heavy consists of 3 Falcon 9 first stages, with a lone second stage in the middle.

Merlin Engine

Each Falon 9 first stage is equipped with 9 Merlin engines. Merlin engines use RP-1 and liquid oxygen as rocket propellants in a gas-generator power cycle.

Starship

Starship is still currently in production/testing phase. Starship will be the next generation of rocket technology, being fully reusable and capable of rapid reusability. Starship stands 400ft tall and will be by far the largest and most powerful rocket in mankinds history. One of the main goals of this rocket will be to handle the large payload capacity required to start a colony on Mars.

Starlink Satellites

Starlink is a satellite internet constellation providing satellite Internet access coverage to most of the Earth. The constellation has grown to over 1,700 satellites through 2021, and will eventually consist of many thousands of mass-produced small satellites in low Earth orbit (LEO), which communicate with designated ground transceivers.
